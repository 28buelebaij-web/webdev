Welcome to Layton, Utah

A fast-growing city tucked between the Wasatch Mountains, and the Great Salt Lake, about 25 miles north of Salt Lake City. Layton, Utah is known for its' immidiate proximity to Hill Air Force Base, the state's largest single-site employer, and its' popular outdoor attraction, Adams Canyon Trail.

Why Layton?

Layton is my favorite place to travel in the summer when it's over 100 degrees in Southern Utah. Layton is a gateway to hiking and skiing in the Wasatch Range, with a quick access to trails, waterfalls, and winter sports.

Quick Facts About Layton

Population: Approximately 86,500 people

Founded: Early 1850's

Annual Rainfal: 18-23 inches

Annual Snowfall: About 52 inches

The city has a young population with a median age in the low 30's

Layton Hills Mall is one of the reigon's largest malls

Despite Utah's desert reputation, Layton has over 40+ parks, and extensive trail systems

Layton By The Seasons

Layton has hot, dry summers, and cold, snowy winters. Located at the foot of the Wasatch Mountains, the city experiences signifigant temperature swings throughout the year, with annual temperatures typically ranging from 24-91 degrees.
